Public Intelligence

1. Operational Security (OpSec)

  • Cover Identities: Operatives maintain cover identities that are thoroughly constructed and backed by appropriate documentation.
  • Communication Security: Use encrypted communication channels and regularly change communication methods to avoid detection.
  • Physical Security: Operatives are trained in surveillance detection and conduct regular counter-surveillance to identify potential threats.
  • Secure Meetings: Arrange meetings in secure locations and vary meeting times and patterns to avoid establishing predictable routines.
    • Always test cover identities by passing through controlled environments, such as airports or border crossings, to ensure their credibility.

2. Counter-Surveillance

  • Behavioral Awareness: Operatives are trained to recognize surveillance tactics and behaviors.
  • Surveillance Detection: Conduct surveillance detection routes (SDRs) to identify and evade surveillance.
  • Use of Disguises: Utilize disguises and alter appearances to evade surveillance.
    • Change appearance by altering hairstyle, clothing, or posture, and avoid drawing attention with unusual or extravagant disguises.

3. Information Security

  • Need-to-Know Basis: Operatives strictly adhere to the principle of sharing information only on a need-to-know basis.
  • Compartmentalization: Information is compartmentalized to limit exposure in case of compromise.
  • Encryption: Use strong encryption for sensitive communications and data storage.
    • Use two-factor authentication (2FA) on all critical accounts and devices to further secure sensitive information.

4. Counter-Intelligence Awareness

  • Identify Threats: Operatives are trained to recognize signs of surveillance or potential compromise.
  • Regular Security Briefings: Stay updated on counter-intelligence tactics and methods used by adversaries.
    • Keep up with the latest in adversarial methods by monitoring news, intelligence reports, and technological advancements in counter-surveillance.

5. Legal and Psychological Resilience

  • Legal Knowledge: Understand the legal consequences of espionage and have contingency plans in place.
  • Psychological Resilience: Operatives undergo psychological training to handle stress and pressure.
    • Regular mental health check-ins and stress management techniques can help maintain psychological resilience in high-pressure situations.

6. Support Network

  • Handler Communication: Maintain secure communication with handlers for guidance and support.
  • Emergency Extraction Plans: Have contingency plans and resources in place for emergency extraction if compromised.
    • Pre-arrange safe houses and escape routes in multiple locations to ensure smooth emergency extraction under varying conditions.

7. Adaptability and Flexibility

  • Adapt to Circumstances: Be prepared to adapt operational plans and methods based on changing circumstances and intelligence.
  • Evaluate Risk vs. Reward: Continually assess the risk versus the potential intelligence gain of operations.
    • Create detailed risk assessments before each mission to ensure quick decision-making when situations change unexpectedly.
